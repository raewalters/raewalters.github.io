APA Writing Style

The majority IWU National & Global (Online, Adult & Graduate) degree programs use the American Psychological Association's (APA) style guide for formatting research papers and citing referenced works.

The following resources give recommendations on APA format.

‍

Current Students

  • OCLS APA 6e GuideStudents using the IWU APA Guide; The Concise Rules of APA Style, 6th ed; or The Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, 6th ed. should download the APA 6e Guide.
  • Academic Writer™is a the official APA Style learning and paper writing tool. 
  • Academic Writer PageTo learn how to create an Academic Writer account and log in.

APA Style Templates

Some of these templates require purchase.

These can assist with writing in APA Style format, but all software is subject to error. Be sure to check your output against official APA rules and the APA edition your class is using.

  • EasyBibIt has a free version or you can pay for the Pro version.
  • EndNoteFree to IWU students and faculty. You will be required to authenticate to download the software.
  • KnightCiteCitation generator provided by Hekman Library, Calvin College. When you first connect, be sure to select APA as your Citation Style, then select the format that you are trying to cite from the left-hand menu.
  • Reference Point SoftwareAPA Templates for $39.95.
  • Slate Citation MachineCitation generator by Chegg Service.
